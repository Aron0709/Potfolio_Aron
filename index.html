    Bachelors in Technology and Science in Mechatronics

    I am a Robotics and Automation Engineer with a Master’s degree from the University of Arizona, where I worked in the SMART LAB under Prof. Hossein Rastgoftar. My research focused on advancing aerial and ground robotics through deformable UAVs, quadcopter–quadruped coordination, and modular ground systems. My thesis centered on the Affine Transformable Unmanned Ground Vehicle (ATUGV), combining deformable hardware with ROS2-based affine control for adaptability and obstacle avoidance. I have published two research papers that reflect my contributions to robotics innovation. The first, “Quadcopter Team Configurable Motion Guided by a Quadruped”, was presented at the 18th International Conference on Control, Automation, Robotics, and Vision (ICARCV), introducing a novel approach to quadcopter motion guided by quadrupeds. The second, “Modeling and Control of a Deformable Copter-Powered Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV)”, developed advanced motion and control models for continuum UAVs, addressing maneuverability and safety challenges. Professionally, I contribute as a Mechatronics Engineer at Steele Robotics, where I design and simulate the G-Bot autonomous golf-ball-picker robot using SolidWorks, Fusion 360, Blender, and NVIDIA Isaac Sim. I also serve as a Robotics Engineer at RoBuildX, developing digital twins of modular microfactories with ROS2 and Ignition Gazebo. My expertise spans Python, C++, MATLAB, ROS/ROS2, SolidWorks, Blender, Isaac Sim, PLC programming, and machine learning tools such as TensorFlow and Scikit-learn. Outside engineering, I bring creativity and teamwork as an active soccer player representing the University of Arizona and as a musician.
