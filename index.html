    Pursuing a Master's in Robotics and Automation at the University of Arizona, I am deeply committed to advancing robotics and automation technologies through hands-on research and development. Currently, I work in the SMART LAB under the mentorship of Prof. Hossein Rastgoftar, contributing to groundbreaking projects that push the boundaries of robotics innovation. My work includes designing quadcopters with configurable motion for precise operation, developing deformable UAVs for enhanced maneuverability and control, and building omni-directional robots with deformable structures tailored for safety and defense applications. As a Research Assistant, I have leveraged my expertise to design modular autonomous systems, such as a deformable ground unit using ROS2 and Python, and optimized motion planning for quadruped-guided UAVs using YOLOv8 and fluid flow navigation. My technical skills span Python, ROS2, TensorFlow, Scikit-learn, Simulink, MATLAB, and expertise in machine learning, statistical analysis, and robotics design. Additionally, I have addressed safety and reliability challenges as a Robotics Technician, ensuring operational compliance with safety protocols. My academic contributions include publications like “Quadcopter Team Configurable Motion Guided by a Quadruped,” accepted at the 18th International Conference on Control, Automation, Robotics, and Vision (ICARCV). Driven by a commitment to innovation and excellence, I aim to continue developing impactful, sustainable solutions that redefine the future of robotics and automation. Additionally, I am an active soccer player, proudly representing the University of Arizona.
